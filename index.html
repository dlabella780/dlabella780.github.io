For as long as I can remember, I used to be the kind of person who spurned the idea of "reading for fun." Despite being a gifted reader from a young age, I would only ever read books that were required for school, and the occasional fiction series I'd taken a passing interest in. However, once I got into college and I was no longer obligated to read, I all but completely stopped reading altogether. Recently, after I began to pursue self-improvement, I started finally understanding that mentality was flawed, and I'm now trying to rectify my past mistakes and make reading a regular habit in my life.

The book I'm currently reading is a book on leadership by Brené Brown titled Dare to Lead, which goes into detail about how having the courage to face things like emotion and vulnerability make for more powerful, compelling forms of leadership.
